IT Resources

This page collects practical, plain-language guidance for everyday technology use. It is designed to help individuals, households, and small businesses understand common IT basics, reduce avoidable risks, and feel more confident about their systems.

  • Clear starting points for reducing stress and improving digital safety.

Password and Account Safety

Good account security is one of the most important parts of protecting your digital life. Small improvements here can reduce a great deal of risk.

  • Use unique passwords for important accounts instead of repeating the same one everywhere.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication whenever it is available, especially for email and financial accounts.
  • Consider using a password manager if keeping track of strong passwords feels overwhelming.
  • Be cautious with password reset messages or login alerts that arrive unexpectedly.

Backup Basics

Backups matter most before something goes wrong. A simple backup plan can protect photos, documents, business files, and irreplaceable personal records from accidents, hardware failure, or malware.

  • Keep at least one backup outside the main device you use every day.
  • External drives are useful, but cloud backups can add another layer of protection.
  • Check backups occasionally to make sure they are actually running and storing the right data.
  • Prioritize the files that would hurt most to lose, not just the largest folders.

Scam and Phishing Awareness

Many modern scams try to create urgency, fear, or confusion. If a message feels pushy, alarming, or too convenient, that is often the first warning sign.

  • Be suspicious of unexpected emails or texts asking you to click links, verify accounts, or send personal information.
  • Watch for slight misspellings in sender names, company names, or website addresses.
  • Do not trust pop-ups that claim your device is infected and demand immediate action.
  • If you are unsure, stop first and verify through the official website or support channel rather than the message itself.

Home Network Basics

Home Wi-Fi now supports far more than laptops and phones. Smart TVs, consoles, cameras, and other connected devices all share the same environment, which makes basic network hygiene more important than ever.

  • Change default router passwords and avoid leaving setup credentials unchanged.
  • Keep router firmware updated when updates are available from the manufacturer.
  • Use strong Wi-Fi encryption and avoid weak or outdated security settings.
  • Consider a guest network for visitors or smart devices when separation makes sense.

Device Care and Maintenance

A little routine care can help devices stay more stable, more secure, and less frustrating to use over time.

  • Install system and application updates regularly rather than putting them off indefinitely.
  • Restart devices from time to time, especially when performance becomes inconsistent.
  • Keep enough free storage available so systems can update and function normally.
  • Pay attention to early warning signs such as unusual noise, repeated crashes, or sudden slowdowns.

Small Business Technology Basics

Even very small businesses benefit from a few foundational habits. Putting reliable basics in place early makes growth easier later.

  • Use business email tied to your own domain instead of relying only on personal accounts.
  • Keep shared documents, account access, and backups organized from the start.
  • Separate personal and business accounts wherever possible.
  • Think about who needs access, what data matters most, and what would happen if a device failed tomorrow.
