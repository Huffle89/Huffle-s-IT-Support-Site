Services

Huffle’s IT Services provides modular, comfort-guided support for home users, veterans, neurodivergent clients, and small businesses that need clear communication, practical solutions, and dependable follow-through.

Work is shaped by real-world experience, industry certifications, and realistic pacing. As a disabled veteran-owned business, Huffle’s is built around both technical best practices and honest limits, with support designed to be useful, respectful, and sustainable.

How Pricing Works

  • All work is quoted after a short consultation so you only pay for what you actually need.
  • Quotes are based on the type of work, the complexity involved, and the estimated time required.
  • You will always review and approve the quote before work begins. No surprise charges.
  • Discounts are available for verified U.S. Military personnel and veterans, as well as verified first responders.

Comfort-Guided Core Support

  • Comfort-Guided IT Support
    Patient, plain-language troubleshooting for home users and small businesses, especially for clients who feel overwhelmed, burned out, anxious, or simply tired of tech support that talks over them.
    • Help with slow or unstable computers, software issues, printers, and general “something is wrong” problems.
    • Support is paced clearly, explained in plain language, and shaped around what is realistic for the client.
    • Especially well suited for neurodivergent users, veterans, elders, and first-time support clients.
  • System Setup and Optimization
    Professional setup and tuning for new or refreshed systems so they are ready for everyday use.
    • Includes operating system setup, driver updates, security configuration, and practical optimization.
    • Available for home systems, remote work devices, and small business workstations.
    • Custom performance tuning for gaming and higher-demand systems is available when needed.
    • Physical limits apply for large or awkward hardware; assistance may be required in some situations.
  • Custom System Builds
    Full custom builds for gaming rigs, family PCs, workstations, and small business systems.
    • Built around your actual goals, workload, and budget discussed during consultation.
    • The parts deposit is the full cost of hardware and is paid upfront before ordering.
    • Non-refundable deposit policy: once parts are ordered, the hardware deposit is non-refundable.
    • If vendor returns are possible, any refund is reduced by shipping and restocking fees.
    • Labor is quoted separately and paid after assembly and testing are complete.
  • Security Consultation
    Structured review of a home or small business environment to identify risks and improve security.
    • Can include device review, account safety, home network review, and practical security guidance.
    • Recommendations are explained in plain language and focused on the way you actually use your systems.
    • Optional follow-up hardening and written recommendations are available when needed.
  • Data Cleanup and Restoration Support
    Help cleaning up cluttered systems, organizing files, and improving data safety where possible.
    • Can include junk-file cleanup, software cleanup, file organization, and drive-health review.
    • Recovery attempts are made where technically appropriate, but full recovery cannot be guaranteed.
    • Often helpful before migrations, rebuilds, or major workstation changes.

Home and Personal Technology Support

  • Device Setup and Troubleshooting
    Setup and support for everyday home technology and small office devices.
    • Includes PCs, laptops, printers, scanners, software setup, and common account or device issues.
    • Useful for new devices, unstable devices, or systems that simply are not behaving as they should.
    • Can also include Wi-Fi, pairing, and basic account configuration support.
  • System Maintenance
    Routine care to keep devices running more smoothly over time.
    • Can include cleanup work, update management, light optimization, and basic maintenance checks.
    • Often paired with data organization, backup guidance, or security review.
    • Designed to reduce buildup before it turns into larger problems.
  • Smart Home Device Support
    Setup and troubleshooting for smart home and connected office devices.
    • Supports smart TVs, speakers, bulbs, plugs, thermostats, cameras, and related devices.
    • Includes account linking, network integration, and simple routines or scenes where appropriate.
    • Mounting, ladder work, and heavy physical installs require client-provided assistance or advisory-only support.
  • Remote and Local Support
    Help delivered remotely or in person depending on what the issue actually requires.
    • Remote support covers most software, configuration, troubleshooting, and consultation work.
    • Local hands-on support is reserved for situations that genuinely need physical presence.
    • All local work is discussed and quoted ahead of time based on complexity and location.
  • Follow-Up Support
    A short follow-up window is included so clients do not feel dropped the moment the job ends.
    • Quick clarification questions are welcome after the main work is complete.
    • Follow-up helps confirm that systems are still behaving as expected.
    • If a new issue appears later, additional support can be scheduled as separate work.

Small Business and Startup Support

  • Foundational Small Business Tech Setup
    Practical technology basics for businesses that need reliable systems without enterprise complexity.
    • Can include device setup, email basics, account structure, and early-stage security planning.
    • Designed for businesses that need stable foundations before expanding further.
    • Helpful for solo operators, home offices, and growing local businesses.
  • Backup and Continuity Planning
    Planning for what matters most if something fails, is lost, or becomes inaccessible.
    • Focuses on critical data, backup habits, and recovery readiness.
    • Built to match the client’s comfort level, workflow, and risk tolerance.
    • Especially important for businesses that cannot afford prolonged downtime.
  • Practical Security and Access Review
    Clear review of business accounts, shared access, and day-to-day safety habits.
    • Useful for businesses that want stronger protection without creating unnecessary friction.
    • Can include password practices, account separation, and safer admin habits.
    • Focused on practical use, not security theater.
